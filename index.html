Top 5 Travel Tips

Read time: 5 minutes

1. Use Monzo and Revolut cards to avoid paying fees to use your card abroad

Monzo and Revolut do not charge fees when you use your card abroad which saves you money on each transaction you make. This is especially useful when travelling to places that prefer card payments over cash.

2. Go during the off season when flights and hotels are cheaper

Places tend to charge more during the school holidays as these are peak holiday times where many people go on holiday. By going during term time you can save hundreds of pounds. Plus there is an added bonus that it tends to be quieter and less busy.

3. Use Walkative to find free walking tours in the city you are visiting

Walking tours tend to take 2-3 hours and you only pay how much you think the tour is worth. It is a relatively cheap way of learning about the history of a new city and an easy way for solo travellers to make new friends. The tours are often run by locals so you can ask for recommendations on what to do and where to eat for the rest of your stay.

"I love travel"- Katie-Ann Murray, 2024

4. Check if your employer has any cashback schemes for flights and hotels

Some employers have benefits schemes where you can have cashback or money off if you book from certain websites. It is always worth knowing if this is available as it can save you lots of money in the longrun.

5. Always compare prices from different websites

You can use websites like Skyscanner to ensure you are getting the cheapest flights, potentially saving you hundreds of pounds.

Top 5 City Breaks

Read time: 5 minutes

1. Paris

If you're in Paris don't forget to go to a bakery and eat some pastries while sat on the grass in front of the Eiffel Tour. Try a Walkative tour if you would like to understand the history of the city and remember to go on the first Sunday of the month if you would like to get free entry to the Louvre.

2. Brussels

Don't forget to try Belgium's delicacies: beer, fries and chocolate. The arcade in the centre has some lovely chocolate shops. I would also recommend going to Pairi Daiza, a zoo 1 hour outside of Brussels, as it is an excellent day out.

3. Oslo

If you're going to Oslo don't forget to get an Oslo pass for free entry into some amazing attractions and free public transport. Don't forget to see the works of Munch and watch the ballet at the Opera House. The Norsk Folkemuseum and the Kon Tiki Museum are particulat highlights.

"I love travel"- Katie-Ann Murray, 2024

4. Milan

If you are going to Milan don't forget to book a day at the Terme Milano spa as it has relaxing saunas and pools. Also, Lake Como is a train away for those who want to sample Italian cuisine with a beautiful view of the lake.

5. Rome

When going to Rome you must see the Colusseum and the Roman Forum. Other places you must visit are the Vatican, Pantheon and the Catacombs of Priscilla.

Top 5 Sunny Holidays

Read time: 5 minutes

1. Rhodes

Rhodes is the perfect place for all holiday makers with the hot weather and beaches for those looking for a relaxing holiday and its heritage sites for those looking to experience culture.

2. Cala en Bosc

Perfect for those wanting a relaxing holiday and to look around the marina.

3. Salou

Why not have some fun and go to PortAventura and Ferrari Land. It's the perfect place for thrill seekers.

"I love travel"- Katie-Ann Murray, 2024

4. Magaluf

Experience history at Palma's castles; have fun at Katmandu theme park; or party at Magaluf's bars and clubs. Magaluf is the perfect holiday destination for people of all ages.

5. Benidorm

The perfect spot for the all inclusive holiday. Don't forget to binge watch Benidorm before you go.
